AIRPLANE-100 APU TASK CARD: OIL LEVEL AND FILTER INSPECTION
Task ID: AP100-49-001  |  ATA: 49-11-01  |  Rev: 02  |  Date: 2024-01-10
Zone: 315 (APU Compartment — Aft Fuselage, Frames 78–85)
Classification: SYNTHETIC DEMO DATA

1. REASON FOR THE JOB

The Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) provides ground and in-flight bleed air and
electrical power. Oil level and filter condition directly affect bearing life
and early detection of internal wear. Required every 600 flight hours per
MPD task AP100-49-001.

NOTE for N123AK: APU S/N APU-10847, TSN 24,100 FH against 30,000 FH life
limit (5,900 FH remaining). APU is approaching final third of service life —
oil condition trending is especially important at this stage.

2. TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT

  - APU oil service unit or clean funnel
  - Oil filter removal tool, P/N APUFILT-100
  - Torque wrench 5–50 Nm
  - Oil sample kit, P/N OSK-7700
  - AuxPower AP422 APU Oil, Spec MIL-PRF-23699F
  - Replacement oil filter, P/N AP422-FILT-A (one per service)
  - O-ring kit, P/N AP422-OR-KIT

3. PRECAUTIONS

  WARNING: APU must be shut down and cool (EGT < 150°C) before opening
  APU compartment. Hot surfaces can cause serious burns.

  WARNING: APU fuel shutoff must be confirmed CLOSED before entering
  APU compartment.

  CAUTION: Capture all drained oil. Spilled oil on APU exhaust duct
  creates a fire hazard.

4. PROCEDURE

  4.1  ACCESS
       a. Confirm APU is shutdown and cool.
       b. Open APU access door — aft fuselage, left side (Zone 315).
          Four quarter-turn fasteners.
       c. Confirm APU FUEL SHUTOFF handle is in CLOSED position (cockpit
         or ground panel). Tag out per local lockout/tagout procedure.

  4.2  OIL LEVEL CHECK
       a. Locate oil level sight glass on APU oil tank (forward left of APU).
       b. Oil level must be between MIN and MAX marks on sight glass.
       c. If below MIN: add oil per step 4.3.
       d. If above MAX: drain excess and investigate — possible combustion
          gas ingestion causing oil foaming.

  4.3  OIL REPLENISHMENT (IF REQUIRED)
       a. Open oil filler cap (top of oil tank, counterclockwise).
       b. Add MIL-PRF-23699F oil in 0.1-liter increments to MAX mark.
       c. Maximum replenishment without investigation: 0.5 liters.
          Excess consumption = raise defect.

  4.4  OIL FILTER REPLACEMENT (EVERY SERVICE)
       a. Place drip tray beneath oil filter housing (lower left of APU).
       b. Using APUFILT-100, remove filter cartridge (counterclockwise).
          Allow oil to drain fully into tray (~300 ml expected).
       c. Inspect old filter element:
            - Cut open element and inspect for metallic debris
            - Any ferrous particles larger than 1 mm = APU hold, notify Engineering
            - Record finding and filter condition in log
       d. Lubricate new filter O-ring with clean APU oil.
       e. Install new filter AP422-FILT-A. Hand-tighten then torque 25 Nm.
       f. Safety wire filter to housing lug (0.032 in. stainless).

  4.5  OIL SAMPLE (RECOMMENDED — N123AK APU CONDITION MONITORING)
       a. Extract 100 ml sample from drain port.
       b. Label: Tail N123AK, APU, Date, APU TSN 24,100 FH.
       c. Submit to lab. Flag if:
            - Iron (Fe) > 40 ppm (bearing wear indicator)
            - Silicon (Si) > 20 ppm (seal degradation indicator)
       d. Trend comparison: if Fe has increased >15 ppm since last sample,
          recommend APU borescope inspection (AP100-49-004).

  4.6  CLOSE-UP AND LEAK CHECK
       a. Confirm oil filler cap installed and torqued (8 Nm), safety wired.
       b. Remove drain tray. Wipe all surfaces clean.
       c. Remove lockout/tagout. Close APU access door and latch.
       d. Start APU per APU Operating Procedure (normal ground start).
          Run for 5 minutes. Shut down.
       e. After cool-down (5 minutes), re-open access door.
          Inspect filter and oil cap for leaks. No leakage permitted.
       f. Re-check oil level — confirm within MIN/MAX.

5. SIGN-OFF

  Certify completion in aircraft maintenance log referencing:
  Task AP100-49-001, AMM ref 49-11-01.
  Record filter condition, oil quantity added, and sample disposition.
