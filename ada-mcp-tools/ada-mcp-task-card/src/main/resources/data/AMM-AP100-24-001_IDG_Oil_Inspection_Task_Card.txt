AIRPLANE-100 AIRCRAFT MAINTENANCE MANUAL
TASK CARD: IDG OIL LEVEL CHECK AND CONDITION INSPECTION
Task ID: AP100-24-001  |  ATA: 24-11-01  |  Rev: 03  |  Date: 2024-08-01
Zone: 410 (Left Engine Pylon) / 420 (Right Engine Pylon)
Classification: SYNTHETIC DEMO DATA

1. REASON FOR THE JOB

The Integrated Drive Generator (IDG) supplies constant-frequency AC electrical
power to the aircraft. Oil level and condition directly affect IDG cooling and
bearing life. Degraded oil causes premature disconnects and potential loss of
one AC electrical bus. This inspection is required every 600 flight hours
per MPD task AP100-24-001.

2. RELATED AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVES

  AD 2025-04782 — IDG Oil Contamination Inspection (Boeing-derived, applied to
  Airplane-100 by similarity). Requires visual and spectrometric inspection of
  IDG oil for metallic particulate above threshold. Compliance required before
  25,500 FH on affected aircraft.

  Note for N123AK: AD 2025-04782 is OPEN. Right IDG (IDG-R-9913) has a crew
  PIREP of temperature fluctuation. Spectrometric sample REQUIRED at this check.

3. TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT REQUIRED

  - IDG oil service cart, P/N GS-IDG-100
  - Oil sample kit, P/N OSK-7700 (for spectrometric analysis)
  - Torque wrench, 5–25 Nm range
  - Safety wire pliers
  - Clean lint-free cloths
  - AeroFictional IDG Oil, Spec MIL-PRF-23699F (or approved equivalent)

4. CONSUMABLES

  IDG oil:  MIL-PRF-23699F class HTS — quantity per step 6.4
  Safety wire: 0.032 in. diameter stainless

5. PRECAUTIONS

  WARNING: Allow IDG to cool for minimum 30 minutes after engine shutdown
  before opening filler cap. Internal pressure and temperature can cause
  hot oil ejection resulting in serious burns.

  CAUTION: Do not mix oil types. Using incorrect oil specification will
  contaminate the IDG and require flush maintenance per AP100-24-009.

  NOTE: If IDG oil appears dark brown, milky, or contains visible metal
  particles, do not return to service. Escalate to AP100-24-004 IDG
  overhaul and notify Engineering.

6. PROCEDURE

  6.1  PREPARATION
       a. Ensure both engines are shut down and cool (EGT < 100°C).
       b. Position maintenance stand to access IDG service panel,
          Zone 410 (left) or Zone 420 (right).
       c. Open IDG service panel — two quarter-turn fasteners.

  6.2  EXTERNAL CONDITION CHECK
       a. Inspect IDG case exterior for oil weeping or staining around
          the oil filler cap, drain plug, and case joint.
       b. Inspect oil cooler lines for chafing, security, and leaks.
       c. Record condition in maintenance log. Any leakage exceeding
          a slow seep requires rectification before return to service.

  6.3  OIL LEVEL CHECK
       a. Remove oil filler/sight glass cap — counterclockwise.
       b. Oil level must be visible in the sight glass, between the
          MIN and MAX marks.
       c. If level is below MIN: add oil per step 6.4.
       d. If level is above MAX: drain excess and investigate cause.
          Overfill can indicate internal coolant contamination.

  6.4  OIL REPLENISHMENT (IF REQUIRED)
       a. Using clean funnel or service cart, add MIL-PRF-23699F oil
          in 0.1-liter increments.
       b. Do not exceed MAX mark. Recheck sight glass after each addition.
       c. Maximum replenishment without further investigation: 0.4 liters.
          If more than 0.4 liters required, raise defect report.

  6.5  OIL SAMPLE (REQUIRED FOR N123AK — AD 2025-04782 COMPLIANCE)
       a. Using oil sample kit OSK-7700, extract 100 ml sample from
          the drain/sample port on the IDG lower case.
       b. Label sample bottle: Aircraft Tail, IDG position (L or R),
          Date, Total FH, and TSI of IDG.
       c. Submit sample to approved spectrometric analysis laboratory
          within 24 hours. Hold aircraft if result exceeds:
            - Iron (Fe): > 50 ppm
            - Copper (Cu): > 30 ppm
            - Tin (Sn): > 15 ppm
       d. Record AD 2025-04782 compliance action in aircraft log.

  6.6  CLOSE-UP
       a. Install filler cap — clockwise to seat, then 1/4 turn.
          Torque: 8–10 Nm.
       b. Safety wire cap to case lug per standard safety wire practice.
       c. Wipe all surfaces clean.
       d. Close and latch IDG service panel.

7. SIGN-OFF

  Certify completion in aircraft maintenance log referencing:
  Task AP100-24-001, AMM ref 24-11-01, and (if applicable) AD 2025-04782.
