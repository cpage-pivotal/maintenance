AIRPLANE-100 AIRCRAFT MAINTENANCE MANUAL
TASK CARD: LANDING GEAR RETRACTION AND EXTENSION FUNCTIONAL TEST
Task ID: AP100-32-004  |  ATA: 32-31-01  |  Rev: 04  |  Date: 2024-06-15
Zone: 730 (Nose Gear Bay) / 740 (Main Gear Bay — Left) / 750 (Main Gear Bay — Right)

1. REASON FOR THE JOB

Full extension and retraction functional test verifies correct operation of
the landing gear actuators, uplocks, downlocks, door sequencing, and position
indication system. Required every 3,000 flight hours per MPD task AP100-32-004,
or any time landing gear maintenance has been performed.

2. RELATED AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVES

  AD 2023-10332 — Landing Gear Retract Actuator Inspection.
  Status on N123AK: COMPLIED 2024-03-18.

  NOTE for N123AK: Maintenance log entry 2026-01-22 records nose gear shimmy
  on landing rollout. Inspect shimmy dampener condition during step 6.3 of
  this task and record findings.

3. PREREQUISITES

  - Aircraft must be on approved jacking points (Jack per AMM 07-11-01)
  - Hydraulic System 1 and System 3 must be pressurized via ground cart
  - Gear bay areas must be clear of personnel and equipment before cycling
  - Two mechanics required minimum (one in cockpit, one observing gear bays)
  - Aircraft must NOT be on jacks outdoors in winds exceeding 25 knots

4. TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT

  - Aircraft jacking kit, P/N JK-AP100-001 (3-point jack)
  - Hydraulic ground cart (3,000 PSI)
  - Gear bay safety locks (red-tagged) — REMOVE before test
  - Nose gear shimmy dampener test kit, P/N SDT-NLG-100
  - Flashlight or inspection light
  - Headset for cockpit/bay communication

5. PRECAUTIONS

  WARNING: NEVER stand beneath or between any gear door or gear leg during
  retraction or extension. Gear loads exceed 40,000 N. Fatal crush hazard.

  WARNING: Confirm all gear bay safety locks are REMOVED and all tools,
  stands, and personnel are CLEAR before operating gear selector.

  CAUTION: Do not cycle landing gear more than 5 times consecutively without
  allowing hydraulic fluid temperature to stabilize below 80°C.

6. PROCEDURE

  6.1  JACK AIRCRAFT
       a. Position jacks per AMM 07-11-01 (nose jack point and two main
          wing jack points).
       b. Raise aircraft until all gear are 150 mm minimum clear of ground.
       c. Confirm aircraft is level — use leveling reference per AMM 08-11-01.

  6.2  CONNECT GROUND HYDRAULICS
       a. Connect ground hydraulic cart to System 1 and System 3 service ports.
       b. Pressurize both systems to 3,000 PSI ± 50 PSI.
       c. Confirm no leaks at connection points before proceeding.

  6.3  NOSE GEAR SHIMMY DAMPENER INSPECTION (N123AK SPECIFIC — PIREP 2026-01-22)
       a. With gear extended, manually compress and extend shimmy dampener
          through full stroke (100 mm).
       b. Resistance must be firm and progressive — no sponginess or free play.
       c. Check dampener fluid level sight glass: must be visible in green band.
       d. Inspect dampener attachment bolts for torque and security.
       e. If any finding is out of tolerance: raise defect, do not proceed
          to retraction test until corrected.
       f. Record findings in maintenance log referencing PIREP 2026-01-22.

  6.4  RETRACTION TEST (3 CYCLES)
       a. Mechanic 2: position in clear area with view of all three gear bays.
          Confirm via headset: "Gear bays clear, ready to retract."
       b. Mechanic 1 (cockpit): confirm gear selector is DOWN.
       c. Mechanic 1: move gear selector to UP.
       d. Observe:
            - All three gear legs retract within 12 seconds
            - All gear doors close fully (no gap > 5 mm)
            - Green gear-down lights extinguish; no unsafe indication
            - No abnormal noise (clunking, binding, or hydraulic surging)
       e. Mechanic 1: move gear selector to DOWN.
       f. Observe:
            - All three gear legs extend and lock within 15 seconds
            - All three green gear-down lights illuminate
            - Gear doors retract to open position (main gear doors)
            - Nose gear doors close (normal — nose gear doors close in down)
       g. Repeat steps (c) through (f) for a total of 3 complete cycles.

  6.5  POSITION INDICATION SYSTEM CHECK
       a. With gear DOWN and locked: verify 3 green, 0 red on gear panel.
       b. Pull gear door proximity switch — verify red unsafe light illuminates
          within 2 seconds. Reset switch.
       c. Confirm EICAS gear position display matches cockpit gear panel.

  6.6  HYDRAULIC LEAK CHECK
       a. After cycling, inspect all actuators and hydraulic lines in gear bays
          for weeping or active leaks.
       b. Maximum allowable seepage: 3 drops per minute per actuator seal.
          Active leakage requires rectification before return to service.

  6.7  LOWER AIRCRAFT AND CLOSE-UP
       a. Confirm gear is DOWN and locked (3 greens).
       b. Install gear bay safety locks (red-tagged) if aircraft remains on jacks.
       c. Disconnect hydraulic ground cart.
       d. Lower aircraft per AMM 07-11-01.
       e. Remove jacks and stow equipment.

7. SIGN-OFF

  Certify completion in aircraft maintenance log referencing:
  Task AP100-32-004, AMM ref 32-31-01.
  Record number of cycles performed and any findings.
