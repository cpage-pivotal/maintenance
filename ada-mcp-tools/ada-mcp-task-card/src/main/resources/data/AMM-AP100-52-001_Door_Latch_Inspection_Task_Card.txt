TASK CARD: DOOR LATCH MECHANISM INSPECTION AND LUBRICATION

1. REASON FOR THE JOB

Cabin door latch mechanisms must operate positively under all conditions
including high cabin differential pressure and emergency arming. Worn or
corroded latches have caused inadvertent door openings and are subject to
mandatory AD action. Required every 600 flight hours per MPD task AP100-52-001.

2. RELATED AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVES

  AD 2025-09505 — Door 1 Latch Mechanism Replacement.
  Applies to Airplane-100-200 series S/N 2200 through 3100.
  N123AK S/N AP100-2847 IS within affected range.
  Status: OPEN. Compliance required before 25,600 FH.
  N123AK current FH: 24,872. Remaining: 728 FH.

  ACTION REQUIRED: During this inspection, the Door 1L and Door 1R latch
  cartridges must be inspected per AD 2025-09505 paragraph (e)(1). If
  cracking is found, cartridges must be replaced before return to service.
  Replacement P/N: LCA-AP100-D1-REV2.

3. TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT

  - Inspection mirror and flashlight
  - Dye penetrant inspection kit, P/N DPK-100 (for AD compliance)
  - Torque wrench 2–15 Nm
  - AeroFictional Door Grease, P/N LUB-D52-200 (or MIL-PRF-81322 equivalent)
  - Clean lint-free applicator cloths
  - Camera for condition documentation

4. PRECAUTIONS

  WARNING: Arm/disarm door slide before opening or closing any cabin door
  during maintenance. Inadvertent slide deployment can cause fatal injury.
  Slides on N123AK Door 1L and Door 1R are OVERDUE for replacement (see
  aircraft technical record ATR-N123AK-2026 Section 2). Flag this finding.

  CAUTION: Do not use petroleum-based lubricants on door seals or girt bar
  mechanism. Causes seal degradation and girt bar corrosion.

5. PROCEDURE — ALL CABIN DOORS (1L, 1R, 2L, 2R, 3L, 3R, 4L, 4R)

  5.1  DISARM DOOR
       a. Confirm with cabin crew (or maintenance lead) that door is DISARMED
          (girt bar in manual/detached position).
       b. Verify visually: girt bar must be hanging free (not attached to floor bar).

  5.2  EXTERNAL VISUAL INSPECTION
       a. Inspect latch handle and handle housing for cracks, deformation,
          or corrosion. Photograph any damage.
       b. Inspect latch rollers (2 per door): rotate manually — must spin
          freely with no roughness. Replace if binding.
       c. Inspect latch hooks: no cracks, no wear groove exceeding 0.5 mm
          on contact face.
       d. Inspect door hinge rollers and guide tracks for wear and debris.

  5.3  LATCH CARTRIDGE INSPECTION — DOOR 1L AND DOOR 1R (AD 2025-09505)
       a. Remove latch cartridge access cover (2 screws, P3 Phillips).
       b. Apply dye penetrant per DPK-100 kit instructions to:
            - Latch cartridge body
            - Cartridge-to-frame mounting flange
       c. Allow developer dwell time 10–15 minutes.
       d. Inspect under white light for linear indications (cracks).
          Any indication on cartridge body or flange = REJECT and replace.
       e. Replacement P/N: LCA-AP100-D1-REV2. Torque to 12 Nm ± 0.5 Nm.
       f. Document AD 2025-09505 compliance. Record findings.

  5.4  LUBRICATION
       a. Apply LUB-D52-200 grease (pea-sized amount) to each of:
            - Latch roller axles (2 per door)
            - Latch hook pivot pins (1 per door)
            - Door hinge roller tracks (wipe on with cloth)
       b. Do NOT grease: handle handle grip surface, door seal, girt bar pin.
       c. Wipe away all excess grease.

  5.5  OPERATION CHECK
       a. From INSIDE aircraft: operate latch handle through full open/close cycle.
          Handle must move with firm, positive action — no binding, no sponginess.
       b. From OUTSIDE aircraft (if access available): confirm door closes flush
          and latch engages positively. Gap between door and frame < 2 mm.
       c. Log any door requiring excess force to latch — reference any prior
          PIREP. (N123AK: PIREP 2025-12-04 for Door 2L — verify correction.)

  5.6  EMERGENCY SLIDE CALENDAR CHECK (N123AK SPECIFIC)
       a. Per ATR-N123AK-2026: Door 1L and Door 1R slides are OVERDUE
          (installed 2014-06-15, life limit 12 years, expired ~2026-06-15).
       b. Raise defect report for slide replacement at next available
          maintenance opportunity. Aircraft may continue operations until
          slide replacement can be scheduled per MEL provisions — confirm
          with Maintenance Control.
       c. Door 2L and Door 2R slides expire 2028-09-01 (2.6 years remaining).
          No immediate action required; schedule at next C-Check.

  5.7  REARM DOOR
       a. Rearm door (attach girt bar to floor bar) as directed by cabin crew
          or maintenance lead.
       b. Confirm green armed indicator is visible in door window.

6. SIGN-OFF

  Certify completion in aircraft maintenance log referencing:
  Task AP100-52-001, AMM ref 52-11-01, and AD 2025-09505 (for doors 1L/1R).
  Record cartridge inspection findings and any defects raised.
